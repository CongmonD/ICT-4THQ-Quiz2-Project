LeBron James

Who Is LeBron?

LeBron James, a basketball player for the Los Angeles Lakers, gained fame in high school as the country's top player. Known for his size, athleticism, and court vision, he won four NBA MVP awards. James won NBA titles with the Miami Heat in 2012 and 2013, and later, he led the Cleveland Cavaliers to their first championship in 2016.

Childhood

Born on December 30, 1984, in Akron, Ohio, James displayed a knack for basketball early on. St. Vincent-St. Mary High School recruited him in 1999, where he accumulated 2,657 points, 892 rebounds, and 523 assists over four years. In his freshman year, James averaged 18 points per game, contributing to the team's Division III state title with a standout performance, earning him various accolades for his skills.
